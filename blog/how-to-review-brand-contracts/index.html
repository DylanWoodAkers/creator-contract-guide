How to Review a Brand Contract in 10 Minutes (Creator's Checklist)

By Sarah Chen · Updated January 2026 · 8 min read

You just got sent a contract. It's 8 pages of legal jargon, and the brand wants it signed by tomorrow.

Don't panic. And don't sign immediately.

Here's a 10-minute process for reviewing any brand contract — even if you have zero legal background.

Before You Start: The Ground Rules

  • Take at least 24 hours. Any brand pressuring you to sign immediately is a red flag.
  • Read the whole thing. Don't just skim. Important terms are often buried.
  • Ask questions. It's normal to request clarification. Professionals do this.
  • Everything is negotiable. The first version is written in the brand's favor. You can push back.

The 10-Minute Review Process

Step 1: Ctrl+F These Words (2 minutes)

Search the document for these danger words:

Search TermWhat to Check
perpetualForever usage rights? Needs premium compensation.
unlimitedUnlimited what? Usage? Revisions? Scope creep alert.
exclusiveLocked out of competitors? For how long? How broad?
terminateHow can either party exit? Is there a kill fee?
indemnifyWho's liable? Should be mutual, not one-sided.
assignCan they transfer your deal to another company?
derivativeCan they modify your content without approval?
worldwideGeographic scope — is this necessary for the deal?

If any of these appear, slow down and read that section carefully.

Step 2: Check the Big 4 (5 minutes)

Every creator contract has four critical areas. Find and review each:

1. What am I creating? (Scope)

  • Specific deliverables listed (number, format, platforms)?
  • Timeline/deadlines clear?
  • Revision limits specified?

2. What can they do with it? (Usage)

  • Platforms specified (not just "all media")?
  • Duration specified (not perpetual by default)?
  • Organic vs. paid ads separated?
  • Whitelisting addressed?

3. What can't I do? (Exclusivity)

  • Specific competitors named (not vague "category")?
  • Duration matches content usage?
  • Premium compensation for exclusivity?

4. How/when do I get paid? (Payment)

  • Total amount clear?
  • Payment timeline specified (Net 15/30)?
  • Payment trigger clear (delivery, posting)?
  • Late fee included?
  • Kill fee for cancellation?

Step 3: Spot the Red Flags (2 minutes)

Quick pass for deal-breakers:

  • One-sided indemnification (you're liable for everything)?
  • "Unlimited revisions" or "as reasonably requested"?
  • No content ownership clause?
  • Payment "upon completion" with no definition?
  • Governing law in a distant state/country?
  • Automatic renewal without notice?

Any checkbox above = negotiate before signing.

Step 4: Know Your Negotiables (1 minute)

Usually flexible:

  • Usage duration (90 days vs. perpetual)
  • Usage scope (organic only vs. paid ads)
  • Exclusivity scope and duration
  • Payment timeline and structure
  • Revision limits
  • Kill fee amount

Usually fixed:

  • General legal boilerplate (force majeure, severability)
  • Governing law (though you can request change)
  • Basic structure of the agreement

Negotiation Email Template

Subject: [Brand] Partnership Agreement - A Few Questions

Hi [Name],

Thanks for sending over the agreement! I'm excited about this partnership. I've reviewed the contract and have a few questions before signing:

1. Usage Rights (Section X): The current terms grant perpetual usage. Could we adjust this to [90 days / 1 year] to align with the campaign timeline? I'm happy to discuss extended usage as an add-on.

2. Exclusivity (Section Y): The exclusivity clause covers [broad category]. Could we narrow this to [specific named competitors] for [duration]?

3. Payment (Section Z): Could we adjust to 50% upon signing and 50% upon delivery, with Net 15 terms?

Let me know your thoughts. Happy to hop on a quick call if easier.

Best,
[Your name]

When to Get Help

Consider professional review when:

  • Deal is over $50,000 — Worth the lawyer cost
  • Unusual terms you don't understand — Ask before signing
  • Major brand with aggressive legal — They have lawyers, maybe you should too
  • Multi-year commitment — Long-term deals warrant extra scrutiny

For most deals under $50K, a solid contract template and this checklist are sufficient.

The "Send Your Own Contract" Power Move

Here's a secret: you don't have to use the brand's contract.

Instead of reviewing and negotiating their brand-favoring terms, send your own creator-friendly contract first. Benefits:

  • Terms start in your favor
  • You look professional and experienced
  • Brands often accept with minor changes
  • Saves hours of negotiation
"Sending my own contract was a game-changer. Brands take me more seriously, and I spend way less time negotiating. Most accept it as-is." — Creator on r/ugc

Quick Reference Checklist

Print this or keep it handy when reviewing contracts:

  • Ctrl+F: perpetual, unlimited, exclusive, indemnify
  • Scope: Specific deliverables and timeline listed
  • Usage: Platform, duration, geography, type specified
  • Exclusivity: Named competitors, matches usage period
  • Payment: Amount, timeline, trigger, late fee, kill fee
  • Revisions: Limited rounds specified
  • Ownership: You retain master files
  • Indemnification: Mutual, not one-sided

Key Takeaways

  • Always take 24+ hours to review — never sign under pressure
  • Ctrl+F danger words: perpetual, unlimited, exclusive, indemnify
  • Check the Big 4: Scope, Usage, Exclusivity, Payment
  • Everything is negotiable — the first draft favors the brand
  • Consider sending your own contract instead of negotiating theirs
